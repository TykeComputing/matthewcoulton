ABOUT

The son of a librarian and growing up in Yorkshire as an only child amongst the books, Matthew Coulton is a multi-disciplinary artist whose work looks to change people's perspective by telling stories through movement, words and images on stage and screen, photograph and microphone.

He has trained at Royal Central School of Speech & Drama, Gothenburg Academy of Music & Drama and Odin Teatret, and works as a director, deviser, actor, mime, puppeteer, movement artist and movement director. He has worked with leading artists and venues such as Complicite, English National Opera, BBC TV & Radio, Bergen National Opera, national and international tours, and films with Timothy Spall and Soarsie Ronan. His own work has featured at the Barbican, the UN Rights of the Child Festival in Sweden, Camden People's Theatre and Theatre N16. He has worked as a movement director for numerous Off-West-End and Standing Ovation award winning and nomatinated productions. He has taught at The Actors' Centre, Royal Central School of Speech & Drama, City Academy, and Guildford School of Acting, where he regularly teaches and directs as an associate.
